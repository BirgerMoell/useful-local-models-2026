Useful Local Models

Studium Assignment Text: Final Report

Submit your final report as a PDF.

Your report should follow the normal course paper format. Follow the

Transactions of the Association for Computational Linguistics style, except for

the course page limit. For the final version, use 4-8 pages plus references

unless the course coordinator announces another limit.

The final version should take peer and supervisor review comments into account.

The grade is based mainly on the final report, but the presentation and how well

you incorporated review feedback may also be considered.

Required Sections

Your paper must include:

  • research question;
  • related work;
  • data and source registry summary;
  • baseline;
  • model and training method;
  • evaluation setup;
  • results;
  • error analysis;
  • significance testing where applicable;
  • local deployment description;
  • ethical considerations;
  • AI assistance statement, if applicable;
  • limitations.

Include an artifact availability statement with enough detail for model training

and local execution to be audited.

Required Appendix Or Supplement

Submit or link:

  • training config;
  • evaluation config;
  • model card;
  • dataset card or data statement;
  • artifact manifest;
  • local run instructions;
  • representative raw outputs or evaluation summary.

Do not include private data, copyrighted material that cannot be redistributed,

or secrets/tokens in the supplement.

If AI tools were used, the AI assistance statement should say which tools were

used, what they helped with, and how the submitted work was checked by the

student.

The work you submit must be written independently in your own words. Follow

standard citation practices. The course policy on plagiarism and writing

assistance applies.
