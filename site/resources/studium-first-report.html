Useful Local Models

Studium Assignment Text: First Report

Submit the first complete version of your term paper according to the course instructions. If the course uses EasyChair or another conference-management system, submit there as instructed; otherwise submit the PDF here.

The first report should be a complete scientific paper. It may still have rough analysis or preliminary results, but no major section should be missing.

Follow the Transactions of the Association for Computational Linguistics style, except for the course page limit. For this first version, use 4-7 pages plus references unless the course coordinator announces another limit.

The paper should include:

  • research question;
  • introduction and motivation;
  • related work;
  • data description;
  • baseline;
  • model and training method;
  • evaluation setup;
  • results;
  • error analysis;
  • conclusion;
  • ethical considerations.

For Useful Local Models, also include:

  • what parameters or component were trained;
  • the local use case;
  • the final or planned artifact format;
  • enough training and evaluation detail for another student to inspect the claim.

Significance testing is obligatory for empirical experiments where it is applicable. If it is not applicable, briefly explain why.

Include a short AI assistance statement if AI tools were used for code, data, experiments, or writing support. State what was used and how you verified the result.

The paper should be anonymized for the first version if peer review is handled as an anonymous review exercise.

The work you submit must be written independently in your own words. Follow standard citation practices. The course policy on plagiarism and writing assistance applies.
