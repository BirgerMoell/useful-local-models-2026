# Studium Assignment Text: Literature Seminars

You will participate in three literature seminars in your theme group.

Before each seminar, read all three assigned papers. During the literature block,
you will present and lead discussion of one paper. Your task is to introduce the
paper and start a useful discussion, not to give a long formal lecture.

Your paper introduction should be at most 5 minutes and should:

- briefly summarize the main question, method, and result;
- point out important assumptions or limitations;
- identify difficult parts of the paper;
- propose questions or themes for discussion.

You are also expected to take active part in discussion of the other papers.

## Theme-Specific Focus

For Useful Local Models, connect each paper to project design:

- What artifact or evidence does the paper make inspectable?
- What data, training, or evaluation choice could a student project adapt?
- What would be a realistic small-model version of the idea?
- What baseline would make the claim credible?

## If You Miss A Literature Seminar

If you miss a seminar where you are not presenting, write a short report and book
a short oral follow-up with your supervisor.

The report should:

- briefly summarize each of the three papers;
- focus on the main points rather than details;
- include at least two discussion questions or themes for each paper.

Recommended length: around half an A4 page per missed seminar.

If you miss the seminar where you were supposed to present, contact your
supervisor to arrange an oral presentation of the paper at a later time.
